# Tim Berners-Lee at CERN

While working at CERN in 1989, Tim Berners-Lee proposed a hypertext system designed to help physicists share research documents efficiently. The system did not replace the Internet — it organized it.

The World Wide Web introduced URLs, HTTP, and HTML as standardized tools for navigating distributed information. Its rapid adoption in the 1990s transformed the Internet into a public medium.
